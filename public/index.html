Simulate.


Joining gives you an opportunity to learn how to simulate fluid, thermodynamic, and kinematic behavior. Some projects include both CFD and FEA of the RPU 1, as well as FEA for the structural elements required for the CubeSat committee. No prior experience is required, join a committee, and we'll teach you!

Create.


Our on-campus lab is equipped with a set of Creality Ender 3D printers, allowing our committees to print everything from prototyping models to components for full scale projects. Additionally, our fume hood and RPI's Design Lab allow for the manufacturing of more complex and varied components. Join a committee and learn how to manufacture using a variety of processes, including molding, 3D printing, and machine processing.

Committees

Rocket

We are building RPU 1, RPI's first liquid bi-propellant rocket engine. Join and help us make history! No prior rocket engine knowledge required.

CubeSat

Lander

The Lander committee is a project based on the 2019 AIAA competition project called Dual Mars Lander. We are working towards designing an automatically controlled landing vehicle. We are currently working with smaller prototypes to develop the control systems and processes for a life sized module.

Rover

The Rover Committee aims to design and develop a versatile rover capable of navigating diverse terrains while conducting soil sampling and data collection (temperature, humidity, and 360º pictures). Some tasks are to invent a soil collection device that we can print and mount to the chassis; along with suspension and wheels catered to traversing rocky and dusty surfaces.

Lunar R&D

The Lunar R&D Committee is researching and writing a paper on the properties of lunar regolith to build structures for a lunar settlement. We are molding regolith mixed with UV-curing or epoxy resin and testing the albedo, impact resistance, and tensile and compressive strengths. All experience levels are welcome and invited to attend!
